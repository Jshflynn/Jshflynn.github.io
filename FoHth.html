Health is all about the Needs of People. I consider 'Foremost Health’ to be about the survival Needs of Others. In other words, bushcraft, survivalism, prepping, etc. There’s a varying probability of local disaster, wherever I am. Floods, storms, earthquakes; things of this nature. So, it only makes sense to have some preparation in mind.

Something everyone can do is get disaster warning apps on their phones. I’ve taken a bushcraft course on Udemy by Thomas Schorr-Kon. He established a survival school, ‘Trackways’, in the UK in 1995 - pretty experienced. And I frequently read John Wiseman’s SAS Survival Handbook (14). If nothing else, it gives you an appreciation for how people used to live and just how poisonous everything is. The US Army’s Survival Manual, known as ‘the FM 21-76’, is free online, by the way.

I’ve also set up something I call ‘The Earth Nest’. It’s a five-tier support system: A main house, a tiny house, a converted van, a bug out bag, and a pocket-kit.

I appreciate that one small trip, you hit your head, and all this preparation was for nothing. But, that’s just the reality of life. I have an inexhaustible will to survive, while I’m in control of things.

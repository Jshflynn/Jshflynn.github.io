■ Sustainer ■

Health is all about the Needs of People. I consider ‘Inmost Health’ to be about the immediate Needs of Myself. I acknowledge there’s always the possibility of a medical emergency. Heart attacks, seizures, sudden injuries; things of this nature. So, I’m well prepared for minor emergencies.

I always have access to a first aid kit and emergency services. I maintain my basic health knowledge, of course. One book I frequently read is the First Aid Manual by the British Red Cross Society (16). There’s a free version online (16) by the Indian Red Cross Society, by the way. I have BLS and CPR certifications. If you’re interested yourself, try firstaidforfree.com. St John Ambulance has clear instructional videos on YouTube, as well.

I go beyond that, of course. My education in Biology enables me to read Harrison’s Principles of Internal Medicine. This enables me to read a lot of MedlinePlus and PubMed articles. I don’t give out medical advice, obviously, but I can understand conversations with physicians and med students. I’m also personally interested in prostheses, organ printing, virotherapy, BCIs, genome editing, and much more.

I’m aware of the specific health needs of everyone in my life; who’s diabetic, who’s asthmatic, who has eye drops, etc. And I’m committed to their Health; people get fitter in my company.

Truth is all about the Mind accurately reflecting the World. I consider ‘Inmost Truth’ to be about reflecting itself. In other words, thought. For this, I use Mathematics.

I teach Mathematics at every level: from preschool to postgraduate. Pick any subject at any level from ‘fraction addition’ to ‘stochastic analysis’. I will instantly provide a self-contained lesson. I aspire to have the speed of von Neumann, the productivity of Euler, and the originality of Ramanujan.

I can produce a paper on a topic in a few hours. My post-graduate knowledge is entirely self-taught.

Beginners
For beginners, I teach from Gelfand's Algebra (93) and Lang's Basic Mathematics (71). I am rapid at mental arithmetic, which young students enjoy and emulate. For example, I can multiply a pair of three-digit numbers almost as soon as someone says them. But, I also have a very high working memory to facilitate this. I can divide a pair of eight-digit numbers mentally with little effort. And I can recall results from years prior.

Intermediates
For intermediates, I teach from Spivak's Calculus (08), as well as Coxeter and Greitzer's Geometry Revisited (67). I also enjoy teaching from classics like Hardy's Course in Pure Mathematics (08) and Euclid's Elements (300 BCE). I’m proficient with Geogebra, SageMath, and other popular software.

Advanced
For those advanced, I teach from Oxford's Notes and the GTM books. I have found Kolmogorov's Mathematics: Its Contents, Methods, and Meaning (99) to be insightful. I've memorised a lot of Bronshtein's Handbook (07), as well.

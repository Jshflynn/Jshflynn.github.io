Peace is all about the Rule of People.
I consider ‘Utmost Peace’ to be about the Rule of All.
In other words, ultimate issues of origins, purpose, and how to live.

Many traditions and religions have arisen to answer these questions.
I try to treat all of them seriously and respectfully.

I have my own ideas about purpose and how we should live.
But, I think you have to be an example first.

Hinduism: I've been to India and lived with a Hindu family. Hinduism is too large to comprehend. I try to read a lot of the Bhagavad Gita in Sanskrit, but rely on Feuerstein's (14) translation for now.

Buddhism: I've been to Lumbini, Bodh Gaya, and Sarnath. I've lived with a Buddhist family in Nepal. My knowledge of Pali could be better, but I'm learning to read the Dhammapada using Norman's (97) translation.

Sikhism: I've been to the takhts. I've learned Punjabi and read the Guru Granth Sahib habitually.

Confucianism: I've been to China and lived with a Chinese family. I also went to the Temple of Confucius. I'm fluent in Mandarin with a TOCFL certificate, but Ancient Chinese is endless. I use Lau's translation of the Analects to help understand it.

Judaism: I've lived with a family in Jerusalem. I've learned a lot of Hebrew, but Ancient Hebrew will require a lifetime of commitment from me. For now, I'm content reading Berlin, Brettler, and Fishbane's (03) Jewish Study Bible.

Christianity: I've done two years of Ancient Greek and Latin. I've been to the Vatican City, too. I read Coogan and Brettler et al.'s Oxford Annotated Bible.

Islam: I've been to Medina, Jerusalem, Istanbul, and Damascus. I've lived with many Muslims before. I'm fluent in Arabic with an ALPT certificate, but Quranic Arabic is almost endless. I memorise a lot of the Quran and Hadith.
